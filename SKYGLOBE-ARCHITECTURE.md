V.2 — The Placement Decision Tree
Ask these in order. The first YES is the home.
```
A new section / service arrives →

1. Does it move a PERSON across a border (visa, permit, admission, job abroad)?
   └── YES → GLOBAL MOBILITY

2. Is it a TRAVEL product or logistics (flight, hotel, insurance, tour, transport)?
   └── YES → TRAVEL SERVICES

3. Is it an EVENT or its verified documentation (conference, summit, training)?
   └── YES → EVENTS & CONFERENCES

4. Is it CONTENT, a TOOL, or TRUST/company info (guide, tool, about, careers)?
   └── YES → KNOWLEDGE HUB

5. Does it move GOODS or facilitate TRADE?              → COMMERCE & TRADE (reserved)
6. Does it move MONEY or provide FUNDING/finance?       → CAPITAL & FINANCE (reserved)
7. Is it large-scale SKILLS / HIRING / LEARNING?        → TALENT & EDUCATION (reserved)

If still unclear → it does NOT ship until the CEO assigns its Division.
Never invent a new top-level item to "make it fit."
```
V.3 — The Three Tiers inside a Division
Within any Division, every item is one of three tiers. This keeps depth
consistent everywhere.
Division — the shelf (e.g. Global Mobility)
Service — a sellable offering (e.g. Student Visa Processing)
Detail — a variant/region of a service (e.g. UK Tier 4, USA F-1)
Rule: a Detail never becomes a nav item; it lives inside its Service.
A Service never becomes a Division; it lives inside its Division.
